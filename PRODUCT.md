# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pinvest LLC team members and approved clients. Context: financial professionals who need polished investor-grade slide decks without hiring a designer or fighting PowerPoint. They open the tool with a specific brief — a pitch, a portfolio update, an IC presentation — and expect the output to look institution-quality.

Secondary: FlowLog-approved users accessing via waitlist. Gated access; onboarding is manual approval by two admins.

## Product Purpose

slides.flowlog generates professional, export-ready presentation decks from a prompt, pasted content, or a template. The output should be indistinguishable from designer-made work. The Pinvest brand system (navy, gold, Cormorant Garamond) is a first-class theme. Access is controlled: users register an email, both admins approve, then the user receives an OTP login link.

Success: the person who receives the exported deck never suspects AI made it.

## Brand Personality

Smart, polished, professional. Quiet confidence. The tool itself is understated; the output does the talking.

## Anti-references

- Old PowerPoint / Word: no legacy aesthetics, no clip-art era buttons, no corporate-dull default styling.
- Generic SaaS builder (Canva): no bubbly icons, rainbow template grids, or approachable-to-a-fault UI.
- Anything that looks "made with AI" on first glance.

## Design Principles

1. **Output quality is the product.** Every design decision should reinforce that what comes out of this tool looks institution-grade.
2. **The Pinvest brand is a first-class citizen.** Navy, gold, and Cormorant Garamond are not just one theme among many; they are the signature.
3. **Gate the front door.** The landing page earns trust before anyone sees the product. Registration and approval flow must feel serious and deliberate, not generic SaaS.
4. **Understate the shell.** The editor chrome disappears; the slide canvas commands attention.
5. **Professional defaults, always.** Every empty state, placeholder, and default value should look like a considered design choice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG mandate, but full color contrast at body text sizes and keyboard-navigable flows throughout. The tool is used in professional settings on desktop.
